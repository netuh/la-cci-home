The Brazilian Conference on Software (CBSoft) is a conference sponsored by the Brazilian Computer Society (SBC). SBC is a civil, non-profit association that brings together students, professors, professionals, researchers, and enthusiasts in Computer Science from all over Brazil, being the largest scientific society of this field in South America. With 44 years of existence, SBC stimulates the scientific and technological development of Brazil by promoting digital inclusion, encouraging teaching, research, and development activities in Computer Science in Brazil, and contributing to the formation of Computer Science professionals with social responsibility.

Registration will be handled by the SBC's ECOS system. However, it is recommended to carefully read the information below to know the conditions and deadlines related to the registration. All registration fees are in American Dollar (R$/BRL).

A full registration allows the presentation of up to 2 papers.

To be included in the conference, a paper requires at least a full-registration and to be presented in the conference (oral or poster). The paper will be included in the proceedings only if at least one of the authors is fully registered in the conference.

The benefits of the registration fee include:

  • Access to all technical sessions, including IEEE LA-CCI and IEEE CIS SSoCIA
  • Access to the exhibition area
  • Conference proceedings
  • Participant materials (bag, booklet, pen, USB drive)
  • Coffee breaks and banquet
  • Social events

Additional Information

There will be a registration fee for students. It is available only available to those who are currently enrolled in undergraduate or graduate courses at Higher Education Institutions and do not apply to post-doc researchers. An electronic proof of the student status with affiliation, participant's name, course which he/she is enrolled at, and current date will be required. The proof must be sent to the registration system. Authors of papers accepted for presentation at the conference must register as full participants.
